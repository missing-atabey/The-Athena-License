* The Athena License
/A Human-Centered, Peace-Oriented, Source-Available Software License/

This license is made for our current era--that of exploitative technologies, unethical sourcing of data, consolidation of technological resources, and of militarization of technologies that could be used to aid the general populace. It's goal is to ensure open access to all who wish to use or contribute to software so long as we ensure that humman brilliance and creativity is enshrined, and that our actions are forbidden from contributing to the aid of our fellow man.

** Inspiration
After seeing someone implement a similar concept and put it up on github, i was motivated to do the same. The difference between Athena and that other license is that Athena focuses on clarity, legal standing, and avoiding subjective sounding terminology, statements, or proposals for violation repercussions. We acknowledge the legal world is complex so we ensured the broadest coverage possible through our wording without sounding vague and unclear.

Athena aims to back developers who care for open-source development but object to the exploitation of their brethren through the usage of generative ai and its development. It aims to be for those who oppose the harm of the innocent worldwide. It is for developers with a moral compass.

We have been inspired in part by the MIT and Apache licenses, and they serve as guides for how to structure our own license without falling into potential legal traps.

** Version (0.2)
In this iteration we have addressed as much as possible to ensure legal clarity and fallbacks in case of unfair litigation. Version 0.2 is the community revision version of the license, allowing all who care for this licensing type to contribute and offer their input.

As of 0.2 Athena remains unrevised by a legal advisor. As soon as we gain traction we will work closely with a legal advisor to ensure Athena is as polished and as legally grounded as we want it to be.

** Usage
Prior to using this license it is highly encouraged to read the entire license's body and ensure u agree with it's philosophy. It isn't super long, I promise :)

If you've decided to utilize Athena on your next project simply "git clone" or download the License into your project directory. Now you'll be free to work and rest assured there's a clear explanation of your licensing terms & conditions.
